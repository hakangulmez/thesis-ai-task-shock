Executive Summary

AI is not just replacing software — it is repricing it. The real disruption is not substitution, but the collapse of pricing power.

An empirical analysis of 143 publicly listed B2B software firms reveals that companies whose products perform tasks LLMs can now replicate grew revenue 41 percentage points more slowly than their infrastructure-oriented counterparts after ChatGPT launched. The gap is widening, not closing.

Two mechanisms are at work. Substitution — customers directly replacing software with AI tools — is visible from early 2023. Commodification — pricing power eroding at renewal even when customers stay — emerged only in 2024, once AI alternatives became credible enough for procurement teams to cite in contract negotiations. Revenue held. Margins fell. Most observers missed it.

The early warning signal is gross margin, not revenue. Margin compression precedes revenue decline by 12–18 months. By the time revenue falls, the pricing power has already been surrendered.

01

The Core Insight: Software as Task Externalizer

The firms most at risk are not failing to adopt AI. They are the ones whose products do tasks that AI can now do directly.

Most AI analysis asks the wrong question. The debate about which companies are adopting AI misses the more fundamental threat: which companies’ products can AI replace entirely.

B2B software firms are not ordinary capital goods — they are task externalizers. They charge customers a monthly subscription to perform cognitive tasks: drafting documents, routing support tickets, tracking sales pipelines, generating reports. The subscription price is a bet that specialized task execution creates durable value. ChatGPT launched in November 2022 and directly tested that bet.

Prior automation displaced workers from cognitive tasks by moving those tasks into software products. Generative AI now displaces the software products that had come to own those tasks. The stack is being disrupted from above.

The Bargaining Power Shift

Before ChatGPT: the software vendor held strong pricing power. A customer who wanted to stop paying had to build the capability internally — expensive, slow, risky. The vendor captured most of the value at renewal.

After ChatGPT: the customer’s outside option improved at near-zero cost. Even if they never actually switch — switching costs, contracts, and workflow dependencies keep them in place — the mere existence of a credible alternative shifts the bargaining outcome at renewal. The vendor makes pricing concessions. The customer stays. Revenue holds. Gross margin falls.

This is why commodification is the more insidious mechanism. You will not see it in revenue figures until it is too late.

02

Three Mechanisms

Mechanism 1Substitution → Revenue Falls

When a product’s core tasks are highly replicable by LLMs and switching costs are low, customers replace it. They cancel, redirect budget to foundation model APIs, or migrate to AI-native competitors.

Most exposed: Standalone point-solution products — document generation, basic analytics, survey builders, simple content schedulers. One set of cognitive tasks, now LLM-replicable.

Signal: Visible from early 2023. The revenue displacement effect grew 57% larger in 2024 as AI capabilities expanded from chatbots to coding agents and agentic workflows.

Mechanism 2Commodification → Margins Compress

When tasks are replicable but switching costs prevent immediate departure, customers do not leave. But at renewal they arrive armed with AI benchmarks and competitor quotes. Vendors concede on price. Revenue holds. Margin falls.

The mechanism is Nash bargaining. The customer’s outside option improved at near-zero cost. Even an unexercised option disciplines vendor pricing once procurement teams trust it enough to cite it. The credibility threshold was crossed in early 2024 — approximately 18 months after ChatGPT launched.

Most exposed: Enterprise SaaS with moderate-to-high switching costs — CRM, marketing automation, customer support, HR platforms.

Mechanism 3Reinforcement → Structural Tailwind

When a product’s core tasks require real-time system access, proprietary data streams, or complex multi-system orchestration, the AI shock creates opportunity rather than threat.

Who benefits: Infrastructure software, security platforms, data warehouses, network monitoring, observability tools. Every AI deployment creates new infrastructure that needs managing.

03

The Evidence

143 publicly listed B2B software companies, quarterly financial data 2019–2025. Task replicability measured from each firm’s pre-2022 product page text — scoring semantic similarity to tasks that LLMs perform well. All treatment variables fixed before November 2022.

1

A 41-point revenue growth gap — and widening

High replicability firms grew revenue 30% post-ChatGPT versus 71% for low replicability firms. Both groups grew — this is differential growth, not decline. The divergence continues to widen through 2025.

2

Margins compress before revenue falls

Gross margin compression was absent in 2022–2023 and strongly significant from 2024 onward — an 18-month lag consistent with the enterprise credibility threshold. Revenue is the lagging indicator; margin is the leading one.

3

$30 million per quarter per standard deviation of exposure

A one standard deviation increase in task replicability is associated with $30 million less quarterly revenue growth per firm. Across a 10-company portfolio, that is $300 million in foregone quarterly revenue versus low-replicability peers.

4

The shock is intensifying, not stabilizing

The revenue displacement effect in 2024 is 57% larger than in 2022–2023. This is not a one-time adjustment — it is an accelerating structural shift.

The Timing Insight: Margins Before Revenue

Gross margin is the canary. Revenue is the coal mine. By the time revenue falls, the pricing power is already gone.

The most actionable finding in this analysis is the 12–18 month lag between margin compression and revenue decline. A company showing stable revenue but declining gross margins in 2024 is not showing resilience — it is showing early-stage commodification. The revenue decline follows.

Q4 2022
ChatGPT launches. Capability exists. Enterprise credibility does not. CIOs are impressed; procurement teams are not yet convinced.
2023
Substitution begins for simple, low-integration tools. Enterprise SaaS customers largely stay — contracts are active, AI alternatives are unproven at scale.
2023–2024
AI alternatives mature. Production deployments accumulate. Procurement teams develop enough confidence to cite AI alternatives in renewal negotiations. The credibility threshold is crossed.
2024
Renewal conversations change character. Vendors make pricing concessions to retain accounts. Revenue holds. Gross margins fall. The margin effect becomes statistically significant in 2024 Q1.
2025+
Some customers migrate. Revenue begins to fall. The substitution that commodification foreshadowed arrives. The financial damage has been accumulating in margins for 12–18 months.

06

Strategic Implications

For Founders

The right question is not “do we have an AI strategy?” — it is “can a customer do our core tasks with ChatGPT this weekend?” If yes, you are in the substitution zone.
In the substitution zone, task performance is no longer a moat. Your moat must be switching costs, proprietary data, or workflow integration depth.
In the commodification zone, your renewal pricing strategy is your most important strategic decision. Your customers already know what AI alternatives cost. Your sales team should too.
Defensible positions: own proprietary data that cannot be replicated, own deep system integrations, own compliance workflows where AI cannot freely operate, own real-time infrastructure.
The worst move: add a generative AI feature to a fundamentally substitutable product and call it an AI company. It buys quarters, not years.

For Investors

Gross margin is the leading indicator. If you are screening for AI risk using revenue metrics, you are 12–18 months behind.
Map every SaaS holding on two axes: task replicability and switching cost depth. These determine which mechanism applies — and how urgently.
High replicability + low switching cost: customers are leaving now. The damage is in progress.
High replicability + high switching cost: customers cannot leave yet, but pricing power is already eroding. Watch the margin line at renewal.
Low replicability + infrastructure depth: secular tailwind. Every AI deployment creates demand for monitoring, security, data, and network infrastructure.
The 41-point revenue growth gap is visible in public financials today. This is not a forecast — it is already in the data.

For Operators at At-Risk Companies

Run a task audit: list every core task your product performs, then ask which ones ChatGPT can do adequately today. That is your exposure map.
Four exits from the commodity trap: deepen integration, build proprietary data, move upmarket into regulated segments, or pivot to AI orchestration — become infrastructure rather than application.
Do not confuse defensive AI features with strategic repositioning. Adding AI content generation to a substitutable product buys quarters, not years.
Rebuild your renewal playbook. In 2024–2025, customers arrive at negotiations with AI benchmarks. Pretending otherwise accelerates churn.

07

What Comes Next

The question is not whether AI will affect your product category — it is when the credibility threshold will be crossed for your specific customer segment.

The substitution effect grew 57% larger in 2024 versus 2022–2023. The automation frontier is advancing from conversational AI into coding agents, multimodal models, and agentic workflows — expanding the replicable task set further with each capability release.

Near Term
2025–2026

Coding agents attack development tooling. AI-native CRM alternatives pressure SMB platforms. Agentic workflows begin replacing process automation products.

Medium Term
2026–2027

Enterprise credibility threshold crossed for agentic AI in compliance workflows. Commodification extends to ERP-adjacent modules. Multi-agent deployment accelerates infrastructure demand.

Long Term
2027+

The replicability frontier advances into currently protected categories. The question is not if, but when — and which firms built infrastructure positions irreplaceable enough to survive it.

The companies that win in an AI-native world will share one characteristic: their value is inseparable from the systems, data, and workflows they operate within — not derived from performing cognitive tasks that an increasingly capable AI can replicate at near-zero cost.

The task shock is not an event. It is a process, and it is accelerating. Every capability release expands the replicable task set. Every expansion shifts more pricing power from vendors to customers. The defensible software companies of 2030 are being separated from the commoditized ones right now — in gross margin lines that most investors are not yet reading correctly.
